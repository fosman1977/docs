Configure and manage integrations with external systems.

## Available Integrations

### Xero
- Financial data synchronisation
- Invoice and billing information
- Payment tracking
- Barrister revenue data

### Plaud.ai
- Meeting recording sync from Plaud devices
- AI-powered transcription
- Meeting intelligence extraction
- Link meetings to leads, clients, and opportunities

### Microsoft Teams
- Notification delivery
- Alert broadcasts
- System status updates

### Google Analytics
- Website traffic analytics
- Lead source attribution
- Marketing campaign tracking

## Managing Integrations

Navigate to **Admin** → **Integrations**

### Connecting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select integration">
    Choose Xero from available options
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authenticate">
    Log in to your Xero account
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorise">
    Grant required permissions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure">
    Set sync options and map barristers to Xero contacts
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Sync Status

Monitor the integration:
- Last sync time
- Records synced
- Errors/warnings

### Troubleshooting

Common issues:
- Authentication expired (Xero tokens expire after 30 days, Plaud after 30 days)
- Permission changes
- API limits reached

<Info>
If you experience persistent issues, re-authenticate in Admin → Integrations.
</Info>

## Integration-Specific Setup

### Xero Setup

1. Click **Connect Xero**
2. Log in to your Xero organisation
3. Grant access to required scopes
4. Map barristers to Xero contacts in **Xero Mappings**

### Plaud.ai Setup

1. Click **Connect Plaud**
2. Log in to your Plaud account
3. Authorise the Chambers Platform
4. Recordings will sync automatically

<Note>
Once connected, clerks can access meeting recordings via **Meetings** in the sidebar. See [Meeting Recordings](/clerk/meetings) for usage guide.
</Note>

### Microsoft Teams Setup

Teams uses incoming webhooks:
1. Create a webhook in your Teams channel
2. Copy the webhook URL
3. Paste into **Admin → Integrations → Teams**

### Google Analytics Setup

1. Create a service account in Google Cloud Console
2. Grant access to your GA4 property
3. Upload the service account JSON in integration settings
